Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 15, 2026

This policy explains what cookies are, which ones watt-guide.com uses, their purpose, and how you can manage or disable them. It complies with the EU ePrivacy Directive, the UK PECR, and CCPA/CPRA where applicable.

1. What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that a website stores on your device when you visit it. Cookies allow the site to remember your preferences, measure usage, or show relevant advertising.

2. Cookies we use

Essential cookies

Required for the site to function. No consent needed. Examples: Cloudflare security cookies, our consent state cookie (wg_consent).

Analytics cookies

Let us measure, in aggregate, how many users visit, which pages are popular, and how people navigate. Provider: Google Analytics or Cloudflare Web Analytics with IP anonymisation.

Advertising cookies

Served by Google AdSense to show contextual or personalised advertising. You can manage preferences at Google Ad Settings.

4. Managing or withdrawing consent

You can accept, reject, or customise cookies through the banner shown on your first visit. You can change your choice at any time by:

  • Clearing cookies for watt-guide.com from your browser settings.
  • Using your browser's cookie controls:
    • Chrome
    • Firefox
    • Safari
    • Edge

5. International data transfers

Some third-party cookies (Google, Cloudflare) may involve transfers of data outside the EEA / UK. These providers are certified under the EU-US and UK-US Data Privacy Framework and use Standard Contractual Clauses.

6. Do Not Track and Global Privacy Control

We respect the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal sent by compliant browsers. If your browser sends GPC, we treat it as a request to opt out of the sale/sharing of personal information under the CCPA/CPRA.

7. Contact

For any cookie-related question, please reach out via the contact page.
